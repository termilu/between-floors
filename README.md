# Between Floors

**Between Floors** is a virtual reality game developed as part of the university course **“Virtual and Augmented Reality.”** The project was created to explore immersive interaction, a creepy atmosphere, and gameplay mechanics specifically designed for VR.

## Overview

In *Between Floors*, the player takes on the role of the last employee leaving an office building late at night. Before ending the shift, you must perform a routine check of the building to make sure everything is in order. This includes simple tasks such as turning off computers, switching off lights in storage rooms, and ensuring that the office is ready to be closed for the night.

While completing these tasks, the player may begin to notice strange and unsettling anomalies appearing throughout the building. Objects may be out of place, environments may change, or unusual events may occur. The player must pay close attention to their surroundings and determine whether something is normal or not.

After each round of inspection, the player uses the elevator to move between floors. Depending on whether anomalies were encountered or not, the player must choose the correct direction. Correct decisions allow the player to progress, while mistakes may lead them to lose the game. The goal is to successfully identify the possible anomalies and get to the final floor.

## Features

- Immersive **virtual reality gameplay**
- Exploration of a **creepy late-night office environment**
- **Anomaly detection mechanics** that challenge the player’s attention to detail
- Simple interaction tasks designed for VR
- Multiple possible outcomes depending on player decisions

## Technologies

This project was developed using common tools and technologies for VR development, including:

- **Unity** game engine
- **C#** for gameplay and interaction logic
- VR headset and controller support
- Custom 3D assets and environmental design

## Installation

1. Clone the repository.
2. Open the project using **Unity Hub** with the correct Unity version.
3. Ensure VR support is enabled and a compatible VR headset is connected.
4. Run the project from the Unity editor or build the application for your target platform.
